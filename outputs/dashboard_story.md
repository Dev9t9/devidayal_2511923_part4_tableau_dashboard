# Dashboard Story — Executive Sales Review

*Written for retail leadership. The dashboard is designed to answer one question: where is profit
being made, where is it leaking, and what should we do about it?*

## 1. Executive summary
The business is **stable and modestly growing** — ₹217.0M in sales over two years at a **15.3%
margin**, up **4.3% year-on-year**. But the headline hides an uneven engine: **Technology earns
almost all the profit, Furniture earns almost none, and deep discounts are quietly turning
profitable orders into losses.** The opportunity is not more sales — it is better-quality sales.

## 2. What is performing well
- **Technology** is the profit engine: ₹153.9M sales at an 18.2% margin, ~84% of all profit, with
  every sub-category (Copiers, Accessories, Phones, Machines) near 18%.
- **Revenue is reliable** — monthly sales never dip below ₹8.9M, and 2025 beat 2024.
- **Regions are evenly profitable** (15.1–15.6% everywhere); South anchors volume and East is the
  most efficient despite being smallest.

## 3. What is underperforming
- **Furniture** sells ₹51.6M but converts it to just ₹3.6M profit (6.9% margin), dragged down by
  **Bookcases (5.7%)** and **Tables (5.7%)**.
- **Home Office**, the largest segment by sales, **returns more** than the others (5.7% vs ~4%).
- **Standard Class** (58% of orders) leaves most customers waiting ~4.7 days.

## 4. What risks are visible
- **Discount leakage is the central risk.** Margin collapses as discounts rise — from 20.6% at
  full price to **−5% beyond a 30% discount, where 72% of orders lose money.** Overall, **288
  orders (6.9%) are already loss-making**, costing ₹574k, concentrated in discounted Furniture.
- **Furniture's high return rate (7.7%)** compounds its weak margin — the same products that earn
  little are also the most likely to come back.

## 5. What opportunities are visible
- **Discount discipline** is a no-cost profit recovery: capping discounts near 20% removes most
  loss-making orders immediately.
- **Mix shift toward Technology** raises blended margin without new customers.
- **Fixing two Furniture sub-categories** (Bookcases, Tables) lifts the whole weakest category.

## 6. Recommended business actions
1. **Cap routine discounts at ~20%**, require sign-off above that, and kill the 31%+ band.
2. **Re-price or re-position Furniture**, starting with Bookcases and Tables (price floors, supplier terms).
3. **Reduce Furniture returns** (better descriptions, packaging, transit protection) and probe Home Office returns.
4. **Lean into Technology** in marketing and assortment; protect the 18% margin.
5. **Stand up a loss-making-order alert** (profit < 0) reviewed monthly.

## 7. Limitations of the dashboard
- It shows **association, not causation** — e.g. discount and profit move together, but other
  factors (product mix, customer type) sit underneath.
- It covers **two years only**, so it cannot separate trend from seasonality with confidence.
- It works at **order/aggregate level** — it flags *where* to look (loss-making Furniture) but not
  the root cause of each return or loss.
- A few fields have minor missing values (customer_rating, campaign_channel) excluded from those views.

## 8. Suggested next analysis
- A **discount-elimination simulation**: how much profit returns if discounts are capped at 20%?
- A **Furniture deep-dive**: product-level margin and return reasons for Bookcases/Tables.
- A **returns root-cause** study for Home Office and First Class orders.
- A controlled **pricing test** to confirm the discount→profit relationship is causal before rolling out caps.

*The charts are meant to be read together: the trend sets the stable backdrop, category and
sub-category views reveal the Technology/Furniture split, the discount-vs-profit view exposes the
leak, and the returns/segment views show who is affected — leading to one coherent action list.*
