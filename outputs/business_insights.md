# Business Insights — Executive Sales Dashboard

Dataset: 4,200 orders, Jan 2024 – Dec 2025. Total sales ₹217.0M, total profit ₹33.3M,
overall margin 15.3%, overall return rate 4.5%. Each insight below follows the required
structure: observation → data evidence → business interpretation → recommended action.

---

## Insight 1 — Sales are stable and growing slowly (Sales trend)
- **Observation:** Monthly sales sit in a tight ₹9–10.5M band across both years with no major spikes or crashes.
- **Data evidence:** 2024 sales ₹106.2M vs 2025 sales ₹110.8M → **+4.3% YoY**. No month falls below ₹8.9M.
- **Interpretation:** The business is healthy and predictable but not accelerating — growth is incremental, not breakout.
- **Action:** Treat the base as reliable for planning, but if leadership wants faster growth, the lever is mix (push high-margin Technology) rather than waiting for organic lift.

## Insight 2 — Technology is the profit engine; Furniture barely pays (Category profitability)
- **Observation:** Technology produces the overwhelming majority of profit while Furniture sells well but earns little.
- **Data evidence:** Technology ₹153.9M sales at **18.2% margin** (₹28.0M profit ≈ 84% of all profit). Furniture ₹51.6M sales at only **6.9% margin** (₹3.6M profit). Office Supplies ₹11.5M at 14.9%.
- **Interpretation:** The company is effectively a technology retailer with a furniture line that adds revenue but very little profit.
- **Action:** Protect and grow Technology; review Furniture pricing, sourcing, and discounting before chasing more Furniture volume.

## Insight 3 — Furniture Bookcases and Tables are the margin drag (Sub-category profitability)
- **Observation:** Within Furniture, two sub-categories pull the whole category margin down.
- **Data evidence:** Bookcases ₹12.5M sales at **5.7% margin** and Tables ₹12.9M at **5.7%** — both far below the 15.3% company average (the worst two of the four Furniture sub-categories). By contrast every Technology sub-category (Copiers, Accessories, Phones, Machines) sits at ~18%.
- **Interpretation:** The Furniture problem is concentrated, not uniform — fixing two sub-categories would lift the whole category.
- **Action:** Run a margin review on Bookcases and Tables (price floors, supplier terms, discount caps) rather than discounting Furniture across the board.

## Insight 4 — Discounts above ~20% destroy profit (Discount impact)  ★ key risk
- **Observation:** Profit falls sharply as discount rises, and beyond 30% most orders lose money.
- **Data evidence:** Margin by discount band — 0%: **20.6%**; 11–20%: 13.6%; 21–30%: **8.0%** with 18.8% of orders losing money; **31%+: −5.0% margin with 71.9% of orders loss-making.** Correlation(discount, profit) = **−0.27**.
- **Interpretation:** Deep discounting is not buying profitable volume — past ~20% it is actively subsidising customers. This is the single clearest profit leak.
- **Action:** Cap routine discounts at ~20%; require approval above that. Even eliminating the 31%+ band would remove most of the loss-making orders.

## Insight 5 — 7% of orders are loss-making and concentrated (Business risk)
- **Observation:** A small slice of orders quietly erodes profit.
- **Data evidence:** **288 orders (6.9%)** post negative profit, totalling **−₹574k**, heavily concentrated in high-discount Furniture.
- **Interpretation:** Losses are not random noise — they cluster where deep discounts meet low-margin Furniture, so they are addressable.
- **Action:** Build a "loss-making order" alert (profit < 0) and review that segment monthly; the fix overlaps with Insights 3 and 4.

## Insight 6 — Shipping speed is a clean trade-off, and Standard dominates (Shipping performance)
- **Observation:** Faster ship modes deliver quicker, as expected, and most volume flows through the slowest mode.
- **Data evidence:** Avg delivery days — Same Day 0.4, First Class 1.8, Second Class 2.7, **Standard Class 4.7**. Standard carries **2,435 of 4,200 orders (58%)** and ₹122.8M sales.
- **Interpretation:** Delivery times are well-behaved, but the bulk of customers wait ~5 days on Standard — a customer-experience lever if speed becomes a differentiator.
- **Action:** Test nudging selected high-value or at-risk customers toward faster modes; monitor whether slow Standard delivery correlates with lower ratings/returns over time.

## Insight 7 — Home Office returns more than other segments (Customer segment behaviour)
- **Observation:** The three segments are similar in size and margin, but differ on returns.
- **Data evidence:** Sales/margin are near-identical (Consumer 15.3%, Corporate 15.2%, Home Office 15.5%), but return rate is **Home Office 5.7% vs Consumer 3.9% / Corporate 4.0%**. Average rating is ~4.06 across all.
- **Interpretation:** Home Office is the most valuable segment by sales (₹74.5M) yet its higher returns shave realised profit — a quality or expectation-setting gap, not a satisfaction-score gap.
- **Action:** Investigate what Home Office customers return (likely Furniture) and tighten product descriptions / fit guidance for that segment.

## Insight 8 — Furniture and First Class shipping drive returns (Return pattern)
- **Observation:** Returns are not uniform — they concentrate by category and, oddly, by ship mode.
- **Data evidence:** Return rate by category — **Furniture 7.7%** vs Office Supplies 3.7% vs Technology 3.0%. By ship mode — **First Class 5.1%** (highest) vs Same Day 2.5% (lowest).
- **Interpretation:** Furniture's high returns compound its weak margin (double penalty), and faster First Class shipments returning more may signal rushed/expectation-mismatched orders.
- **Action:** Prioritise Furniture return-reduction (descriptions, packaging, damage in transit); audit First Class orders for what is being sent back.

## Insight 9 — South leads sales but regions are evenly matched (Regional performance)
- **Observation:** Sales differ by region while margins are nearly identical.
- **Data evidence:** South ₹64.7M, North ₹54.6M, West ₹48.9M, East ₹48.9M; margins all 15.1–15.6%. East has the highest return rate (4.9%) and, despite lowest sales, the best margin (15.6%).
- **Interpretation:** No region is structurally broken; the gap is volume/coverage, not profitability. South is the anchor; East is small but efficient.
- **Action:** Study what makes East efficient and replicate it; treat South as the model for scale and protect its base.

---

### Opportunity summary
The biggest, fastest win is **discount discipline** (Insight 4) — it directly recovers profit with no new investment. The biggest **strategic** question is the **Technology-vs-Furniture mix** (Insights 2, 3, 8): Furniture ties up revenue at low margin and high returns, so re-pricing or re-positioning it is the largest profit-quality lever.
